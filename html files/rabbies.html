Rabies Disease Information

Rabies is a viral disease that affects the central nervous system of mammals, including humans. It is transmitted through the saliva of infected animals, usually through a bite.

Symptoms

  • Flu-like symptoms
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Weakness
  • Discomfort or pain at the site of the bite
  • Agitation
  • Confusion
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Excessive salivation
  • Seizures
  • Paralysis

Treatment

If you suspect you have been exposed to rabies, it is crucial to seek medical attention immediately. Post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) is recommended for anyone who may have been exposed to the virus, which typically includes a series of rabies vaccinations along with rabies immune globulin.

Treatment for rabies exposure is most effective when administered soon after the exposure occurs. However, once symptoms of rabies develop, the disease is almost always fatal.

Vaccination of pets, especially dogs and cats, is important for preventing rabies transmission to humans.
